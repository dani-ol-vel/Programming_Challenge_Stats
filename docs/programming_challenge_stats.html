The results suggest significantly negative association between name length and covid cases (p= .023). However, the model is poorly fitted(Adjusted R-squared: 0.0001028). The relationship between name length and covid cases cannot be causal. Based on the Bradford Hill’s criteria for causation, a plausible mechanism between cause and effect is important for causal implications. Apparently there lacks a plausibility in the examined relationship.

There is a positive significant correlation (p = 0.0408) between the day of a week and number of deaths caused by COVID-19 in Poland. This correlation doesn’t seem to occur for many of the other European countries, with focus on Slavic countries, countries with a similar population, as well as countries with similar ratings of anti-COVID response - it seem to be accidental. In other words, in order to obtain this correlation, the country has been cherry-picked from the pool of other countries, that don’t show any similar correlation, and the relationship lacks plausible causality.
